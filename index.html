About Me

Who am I?


Hello, my name is Malcolm Gilmore, and I am an Embedded Systems Engineer with a Bachelor of Electrical Engineering and a Minor in Computer Science from Morgan State University. I have a diverse range of experience across firmware validation, software engineering, and embedded systems, having worked with companies like Intel Corporation, Johns Hopkins APL, Northrop Grumman, and B2B Mobile Auction. During my time as a Firmware Validation Intern at Intel, I collaborated with technicians to create customized applications and design firmware validation tests for SSDs and SSHDs.

I also worked as a Software Engineer at Johns Hopkins APL, where I developed a web application to facilitate collaboration between scientists and engineers. My internship at Northrop Grumman involved automating server processes through BASH and Python scripts, while my role at Crosswire Studios allowed me to assist in developing interactive puzzles using the Raspberry Pi platform. I have a passion for technology and have worked on projects such as openDrop, a drone delivery network with a web application, and the LSI Lunar Base Initiative, where I helped create system dynamics models to support lunar base research. Additionally, I created and taught an IT & Networking course for the Black Cybersecurity Association.

I am highly skilled in programming languages like Python, C++, Java, and JavaScript, and have extensive experience working with Linux, OSX, and Windows operating systems. My technical expertise includes scripting with BASH, PowerShell, and CSHELL, as well as strong knowledge in computer networking.
